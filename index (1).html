About

Hydraulic experiments, CFD and AI for scour, sediment transport and coastal hazard mitigation.

I am a Research Fellow at the National University of Singapore. My research addresses coastal and hydraulic engineering problems involving local scour, sediment transport, turbulence, morphodynamics and infrastructure stability. I am particularly interested in combining physically based modelling with laboratory measurements and data driven methods to support safer and more sustainable coastal and water infrastructure.

My PhD research at the University of Auckland focused on scour countermeasures for offshore wind turbine monopile foundations. My current work at NUS focuses on CFD modelling of coastal erosion and sediment transport.

Research interests

Four connected themes define my current research portfolio.

Scour countermeasures for offshore wind foundations

Experimental and theoretical studies of collar protection, riprap armour, bed solidification and bio cemented beds for monopile foundations.

Bridge inundation, pressure scour and hydrodynamic forces

Flood induced bridge deck submergence, vertical contraction scour, uplift forces and prediction equations for engineering design.

CFD, turbulence closure and morphodynamic simulation

Numerical modelling of hydrodynamics, turbulent sediment diffusion, suspended sediment and bed evolution using OpenFOAM based tools.

Machine learning for water hazards

Data driven reconstruction and prediction for scour, streambed evolution and geophysical systems with sparse measurements.

Nature based and hybrid coastal defence

Wave attenuation, mangrove planter systems and engineering solutions that combine structural stability with ecological benefit.

Ship waves and coastal sediment dynamics

Wave structure and wave sediment interactions relevant to navigation, nearshore erosion and coastal infrastructure protection.
